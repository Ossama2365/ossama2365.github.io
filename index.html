Project Idea
As a major fan of basketball and a practitioner of frequent backyard basketball games, there is certainly room for an exciting and innovative IT creation to step forward. A product that is able to act as a customizable at-home scoreboard for all aspiring hoopers to utilize. I have seen a few at-home scoreboards on the market today that offer the ability to keep track of scores, time or fouls at the press of a button on a small device, usually fixed onto the basketball pole. This project idea is for a much larger scoreboard with multiple features that is fixed onto the back of any see-through backboard. This would not have been possible a few years ago as plastic backboards were quite common on portable basketball systems. Today the trend has shifted heavily toward a polycarbonate or glass backboard as it allows for much sturdier rebounding which better simulates a professional or amateur basketball setup. This scoreboard would be customizable through a companion application. You would be able to change details such as team names, points per bucket, points required to win, time limit and the interface in which these details are displayed. Operation of the scoreboard during active play would be through speech recognition. Through speech the device would have as little burden on the players and disruption of play as possible. Simply announcing the points, fouls or re-matching would make for quick uninterrupted basketball which is what no other at-home scoreboard currently offers. This device would also be located within the most natural location for basketball players to be looking at, the rim. The scoreboard would also have in built speakers to play sound effects intrinsic in the score board’s functionality. For instance, at the conversion of a basket and the subsequent call of “2 points B” (Team B) for the software, a sound prompt will activate to indicate the reception of the call out. This will ensure the scores are accurately recorded and maintained. Of course, there are plenty of potential concerns. Many different backboards come in different shapes and sizes and while this device would look seamless on some systems it may be partially blocked or may not fit on some backboards at all. There is also the aforementioned community of plastic, colored or opaque backboards that are incompatible. Although they are certainly the minority of basketball systems, they do still render this invention useless. Working with speech recognition software can be tricky and could pose a threat to the effectiveness of this device however the companion app will also have the ability to record the same data that may need to be manually inputted thus significantly reducing the usefulness, originality or effectiveness of the device. There is also the question of sturdiness given the dunking that could often occur. A lot of individuals get at-home basketball systems to lower the rim and live out their dream of dunking, so the sturdiness of the system could dictate the wellbeing of the scoreboard. A similar device has been created which offers a portable scoreboard to be placed anywhere and is purely controlled by a companion application. Meaning a third party will be required to make use of it unless active play is to be halted after every basket which is not ideal. It is clearly designed to accompany pick-up games and offer a variety of features that can support such an environment such as a mic port, internal speakers to connect smartphones to and it can even act as an amplifier for musical instruments. As far as my research tells me this would be the biggest competitor to my projected idea beside the archaic digital clocks and portable score counting devices of the past. As mentioned above the scoreboard’s main utility is as a display for information. That information would be inputted through speech recognition software available on the companion app and then displayed through the scoreboard. The scoreboard itself may require technology such as a Raspberry Pi to support the processes intrinsic in the scoreboard. The apps functionality would ideally include as many customizations as possible such as background color, font, font color and perhaps themes. Accurate speech recognition software would be the most important aspect of this project to successfully acquire. With the completion of this project we would most likely see the most modern, hands-off, and intuitive at-home scoreboard on the market. The successful operation of the speech recognition software would allow players to never have to keep score. It would also prosper the use of timed games which, today, is not a very common way of playing backyard basketball due to its inconvenient nature. Having a scoreboard front and center with little set-up required would foster an otherwise inconvenient game mode. Timed games would also instill a sense of urgent competitiveness as time whittled down to the final minute, similar to the intenseness that often follows a player reaching match point. A product like this could become a staple of convenience to almost all aspiring recreational basketball players and could eliminate one more distraction to allow ballers, to just ball.
